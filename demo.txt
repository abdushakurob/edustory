This introductory physics course provides a comprehensive foundation in the fundamental principles governing the natural world. Students will explore classical mechanics, including motion, forces, energy, and momentum, as well as the basics of thermodynamics, waves, and fluid dynamics. Emphasis is placed on developing problem-solving skills through quantitative analysis and experimentation. The course integrates real-world applications to demonstrate how physics explains phenomena ranging from planetary motion to everyday mechanical systems, preparing students for advanced study in physics, engineering, or related scientific fields.
